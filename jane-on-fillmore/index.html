Our Story

A corner of Fillmore that smells like butter and coffee

There's a stretch of Fillmore Street where the neighborhood slows down just a little — where people actually stop instead of rushing past. Jane sits right in that stretch, between California and Sacramento, and it has a way of pulling you in whether you planned to stop or not. The smell hits you first. Then the line. Then you realize you've been coming here every week for two years.

What makes Jane work isn't a gimmick or a concept — it's the consistency. The pastries are made carefully, the coffee is taken seriously, and the room has the kind of easy warmth that's genuinely hard to manufacture. The staff moves with the kind of practiced calm you only see in places that have been doing this long enough to stop trying too hard.

People come in alone with a laptop and end up staying longer than they meant to. Couples make it their Saturday ritual. Regulars greet each other by accident, strangers share the little marble tables without awkwardness. It's not trying to be a community hub — it just became one, the way good neighborhood places do, quietly and without fanfare.

"I've walked past Jane probably a thousand times but didn't actually go in until a friend dragged me. Now it's embarrassing how often I'm here. The morning bun is genuinely one of the best things I've eaten in this city and the coffee is exactly as strong as I need it to be at 7:30am. The staff remembered my order after like three visits, which felt either impressive or slightly alarming."

Pastries & Baked Goods

The pastry case is the first thing you see when you walk in, and it's the reason people make the detour. Croissants, scones, morning buns — made in a way that makes you wish your commute was longer.

Coffee & Tea

The espresso is pulled properly and the drip is never sitting too long — small things that matter more than most places realize. Whether you want a flat white to go or something to linger over, the coffee here earns its place in your morning.

Breakfast & Brunch

On weekends especially, Jane becomes the kind of place where brunch doesn't feel like a production — just good food, a relaxed pace, and no pressure to give up your table. It's a proper sit-down morning without the fuss.
