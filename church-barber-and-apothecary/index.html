Our Story

Where Octavia Street Gets a Little Sharper

There's a particular kind of San Francisco business that doesn't feel like it's trying to be anything other than what it is. Church Barber & Apothecary, sitting quietly on Octavia between Ivy and Hayes, is exactly that. The name alone tells you something — a barbershop that takes the craft seriously enough to stock the shelves with good product, run the place with intention, and treat a Tuesday afternoon haircut like it actually matters.

The apothecary side isn't an afterthought. It reflects a real belief that the stuff you put in your hair after a great cut should be just as considered as the cut itself. The barbers here know product the way a good cook knows ingredients — they'll tell you what works for your hair type, what doesn't, and they're not trying to upsell you on something you don't need. That kind of honesty tends to keep people coming back.

Hayes Valley has no shortage of places to spend your money on a Saturday. But there's something about Church that people seem to return to again and again — the 87 reviews on Yelp averaging 4.6 stars tell part of that story, but the real version is simpler: it's a shop where someone actually pays attention to your hair, remembers how you like it, and sends you out the door looking like yourself, only better. In a neighborhood full of beautiful things, that kind of straightforward care stands out.

What Happens in the Chair

Classic barbering, done right — no rush, no shortcuts, good product when you need it.

Haircut

This is the foundation — a real conversation about what you want, followed by a barber who actually listens. Whether you want something tight and clean or a looser shape with some texture, they'll get it right and make sure you know how to maintain it at home.

Beard Trim & Shaping

Not just a cleanup, but an honest assessment of what shape actually works with your face. You'll leave with edges that look intentional, not like you got ambushed with a razor.

Apothecary Products

The shelves are stocked with the kind of grooming products you won't find at a chain drugstore — pomades, balms, waxes, oils, all curated with a point of view. Ask the barber what's right for your hair type and they'll give you a straight answer.
