Our Story

Where 3rd Street Gets Its Act Together

The Dogpatch neighborhood has changed a lot over the years — warehouses turned into breweries, empty lots turned into dog parks — but some things still need to stay the same. A good barbershop should feel like a barbershop. Dogpatch Barber and Shave South landed on 3rd Street and quietly became exactly that: a place where the clipper hum is familiar, the conversation is real, and nobody's trying to upsell you a scalp serum.

The craft here is old-school in the best sense. Straight razor shaves done slowly and deliberately. Fades that actually fade. Haircuts that account for how your hair grows, not just how it looks on the chair. The barbers here pay attention — to the cowlick you've had since third grade, to the fact that you've got a job interview on Friday, to whether you want to talk or just decompress for twenty minutes.

Nearly a hundred Yelp reviews and counting, and most of them say some version of the same thing: I found my guy. That's what a neighborhood barbershop is supposed to do. Not wow you once and hope you forget the details — but earn a regular spot in your routine. Somewhere between 22nd and 23rd, a lot of people in San Francisco found theirs.

"I've been bouncing between barbers for two years since I moved to the city and finally just stopped looking. This place is it. My barber remembered exactly what I asked for the first time and just did it again on my second visit without me saying a word. That's rare. The straight razor shave they did before a wedding I was in was also outstanding — smooth for two days straight."

What We Do

Traditional barbering, done right — haircuts, fades, and straight razor shaves for people who care about the details

Haircut

Not a trim, not a style consultation — a proper haircut from someone who knows what they're doing. You'll sit down, say a few words about what you're going for, and leave with a cut that actually holds up through the week.

Fade

The kind of fade that takes patience to do well and shows immediately when it's done badly. Here, the taper is clean, the blend is smooth, and you won't find yourself checking the back of your head with two mirrors feeling vaguely disappointed.

Straight Razor Shave

Hot towel, straight razor, no rush. This is the shave you give yourself when you have somewhere important to be — or when you just want to feel like a person again after a long week.
