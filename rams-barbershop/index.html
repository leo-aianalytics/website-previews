Our Story

A neighborhood institution on MacArthur Boulevard

Ram's Barbershop sits right there on MacArthur Boulevard, between the bodega and the laundromat, the kind of place you pass a hundred times before you finally walk in. When you do, you'll find a small, focused shop that's been doing one thing consistently: giving people haircuts they actually want. No fancy waiting area, no playlist trying too hard — just barbers who've been doing this long enough to know the difference between a trim and a fade.

The regulars here aren't random. They come back because they trust the hands holding the clippers. You'll see high schoolers getting lined up before the weekend, older guys keeping their shape tight, families bringing their kids in for their first real haircut. That kind of loyalty doesn't come from an Instagram ad — it comes from doing solid work, week after week, in a place that feels like it belongs to the neighborhood.

Walk in on a Saturday morning and you'll understand what keeps people coming back. There's no pretense here, just the sound of clippers, a barber focused on getting it right, and customers who know they're in good hands. That's Ram's — it's not fancy, but it's real, and in Oakland, that matters.
