Our Story

Where the Ferry Building smells like it should

There's a moment every morning at the Ferry Building — somewhere around 7am, when the fog is still sitting on the bay and the commuters are just starting to flow through — when the smell hits you before you even see the shop. That's Acme. Flour, crust, that faintly tangy warmth of a real sourdough culture doing its slow, unhurried work. It stops people mid-stride.

Acme's roots go back to the 1980s when founder Steve Sullivan started baking in a way that was, at the time, quietly radical — using wild yeast starters, long fermentation times, and the kind of European-style technique that most American bakeries had forgotten or never known. The Ferry Building location became a landmark not because of marketing, but because the bread kept being exactly what it promised to be: honest, substantial, alive.

Over a thousand people have left reviews trying to put their finger on why they keep coming back, and the answers are remarkably consistent — it's not just the loaves, it's the ritual. The Saturday morning walk to the waterfront, the heft of a paper bag on the way home, the first slice still slightly warm. Acme doesn't sell an experience. It just bakes really good bread, and the experience shows up on its own.

"I've been stopping here on my way through the Ferry Building at least twice a week for three years. The pain de campagne is the best thing I've eaten on a regular basis in this city — and I've tried a lot of bread. There's something about the crust-to-crumb ratio that I genuinely cannot explain but also cannot live without. My partner now requests it by name for every dinner party."

What We Bake

Slow fermentation, real ingredients, nothing that needs a paragraph of explanation

Sourdough & Country Loaves

The backbone of everything here — wide, domed loaves with a crackling crust and a crumb that holds up to whatever you put on it. These are the loaves people build their weekends around, picked up fresh and carried home like something worth protecting.

Baguettes & Specialty Breads

Slender, properly blistered baguettes that actually crunch when you break them, alongside seasonal and specialty options that rotate through depending on what's good and what the bakers feel like making. No two weeks are exactly the same.

Pastries & Morning Bakes

On the sweeter side of the counter you'll find the kind of morning pastries that make a strong argument for arriving early — buttery, flaky, and gone by mid-morning most days. Come before 9am if something specific is on your mind.
