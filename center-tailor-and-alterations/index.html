Our Story

Three generations of needlework on Castro Street

Maria opened Center Tailor back in 2008 in a narrow storefront that used to house a small café. She'd learned to sew from her grandmother in El Salvador, then spent years working for high-end boutiques in San Francisco before deciding Mountain View needed a place where people could get honest alterations without the downtown markup.

What started as Maria working alone with her Singer machine has grown into a family operation. Her daughter Carmen handles the front desk and intake, while Maria's hands still do the delicate work—taking in waistlines, shortening sleeves, and somehow making off-the-rack clothes look custom-made.

The shop stays busy because word travels fast in Mountain View. Tech workers bring in pants that need hemming, brides drive over from Palo Alto for dress fittings, and longtime customers trust Maria with their favorite pieces that just need a little refresh. It's the kind of place where they remember your measurements and always ask about your kids.

What We Perfect

Every piece gets the attention it deserves, from quick hems to complete restyling

Everyday Alterations

Hems, waistbands, sleeves—the bread and butter work that makes your regular clothes fit like they were made for you. Most pieces are ready in a few days, and Maria always checks the fit twice.

Special Occasion Work

Wedding dresses, formal wear, and those pieces that absolutely have to be perfect. Carmen schedules extra time for fittings, and Maria treats every gown like it's for her own daughter's wedding.

Repairs & Restyling

Bringing beloved pieces back to life—replacing zippers, patching tears, or completely reimagining something that's been hanging in your closet. If the fabric is good, Maria can probably save it.
