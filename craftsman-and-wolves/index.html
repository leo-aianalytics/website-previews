Our Story

The bakery that changed what Valencia Street smelled like

Before Craftsman and Wolves opened at 746 Valencia, this stretch between 18th and 19th was already busy — taquerias, bookshops, the usual Mission shuffle. But something shifted when this place arrived. The smell of laminated dough and dark coffee started drifting out onto the sidewalk in the early morning hours, and suddenly people had a reason to slow down. Regulars started showing up before 8am, coffee in hand, peering into the glass cases like they were looking at something in a museum.

What sets the baking here apart isn't just technique — though there's plenty of that — it's the sense that someone in that kitchen is genuinely having fun. The Rebel Within, a savory muffin with a whole soft-cooked egg baked inside, became something of a legend. But it's not just the famous stuff. The seasonal tarts change, the croissants rotate, the coffee program keeps up. There's a restlessness to the menu that keeps even longtime regulars checking the case each visit, never quite sure what they'll find.

Over 1,900 Yelp reviews in, people still write about their first bite here like it was a small revelation. That doesn't happen at a place that's just going through the motions. It happens at a place where someone cares deeply about the difference between good and extraordinary — and quietly refuses to settle. For a lot of Mission neighbors, weekend mornings now have a shape: Valencia Street, a warm bag, a decent cup of coffee, and whatever's in the case today.

"I've been to a lot of fancy bakeries in SF and this one still surprises me. Came in on a Saturday around 9am and the case was already half depleted — which tells you everything. Got the Rebel Within muffin and a cortado. The egg inside the muffin is somehow perfectly cooked every time. I don't know how they do it. Will never stop coming here."

What Comes Out of the Kitchen

Baked fresh each morning, gone by mid-afternoon — no two visits are quite the same

Pastries & Viennoiserie

The croissants here are the kind that leave flakes on your shirt and you don't mind at all. From classic butter to more inventive laminated creations, the pastry case is where Craftsman and Wolves earns its reputation — made fresh each morning and usually selling out well before closing.

Cakes & Patisserie

Whether it's a precisely assembled entremet or a slice of something seasonal pulled from the cold case, the patisserie work here sits in that rare space between technically impressive and genuinely delicious. It's not just pretty — it tastes like someone made it carefully, for you.

Coffee & Drinks

The coffee is good enough to hold its own at a place where the food could easily overshadow it — and that's saying something. Whether you're grabbing an espresso to go or settling in with a latte and a pastry, the drinks are thoughtful, well-made, and worth the walk up Valencia.
