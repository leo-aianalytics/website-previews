Our Story

A family trade on 9th Avenue

In 1969, Issa Shamiya chose 9th Avenue in the Inner Sunset to open a small barbershop. He didn't run ads or launch promotions — he just gave good haircuts. Word spread on the block, then the neighborhood, then across the city.

When his son Jay grew up watching his father work, the craft felt like a natural calling. Jay now runs the chair, bringing his own style while honoring his father's standards: take your time, listen to what the customer wants, and never rush a cut.

Over five decades later, families who first came in as kids now bring their own children. That's not something you build with marketing — it's something you earn, one haircut at a time.
