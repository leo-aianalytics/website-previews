Our Story

A corner of Columbus that time forgot to change

There's a moment most mornings, somewhere around 8am, when Columbus Avenue smells like coffee and warm pastry dough and it's hard to imagine being anywhere else. Stella Pastry & Cafe sits right in the middle of all that, at the corner of Vallejo, in a neighborhood that has been feeding writers and fishermen and immigrants and tourists for well over a century. Stella has been part of that story for a long time — long enough that some customers who come in now remember coming in as children with their own parents.

The case up front is the kind that makes you stop mid-sentence. Cannoli, sfogliatelle, a few things you might not recognize but will absolutely point at and say 'one of those.' The sacripantina is the one people talk about most — an Italian sponge cake soaked in rum and Marsala, layered with zabaglione cream, and utterly unlike anything you'd find outside of a Genoese kitchen or, apparently, this particular stretch of North Beach. It's the kind of recipe that doesn't get fussed with.

What keeps people coming back — over 1,700 of them have said something about it on Yelp alone — isn't just the pastry, though the pastry is genuinely great. It's the way the place holds its shape. The espresso is pulled the same way it was pulled years ago. The tables are close together. Nobody's rushing you. In a city that reinvents itself every few years, Stella has quietly decided not to, and the neighborhood is grateful for it.

"I've been coming here since I moved to North Beach six years ago and the sacripantina cake is still the best thing I've eaten in San Francisco. Not exaggerating. I've brought visitors here specifically to try it and everyone has the same reaction — they go quiet for a second. The espresso is also exactly right. This is my default answer when anyone asks me for a cafe recommendation."

What comes out of the kitchen

Italian pastry, espresso, and a few things you'll come back for specifically

Italian Pastry & Baked Goods

The display case runs the length of the counter and changes with the seasons, but the classics — cannoli, sfogliatelle, almond cookies — are always there. Come in without a plan and just point at whatever looks best; you won't regret it.

Sacripantina Cake

This is the thing Stella is known for, and it earns it — a Genoese rum cake with layers of zabaglione cream that you can order by the slice or as a whole cake for a birthday or just because it's Tuesday. Call ahead if you want a full cake; they do run out.

Coffee & Espresso

The cafe side of Stella is low-key and genuinely good — proper espresso, cappuccino the way it's supposed to be made, and a place to sit and drink it without someone hovering. On Friday and Saturday nights they stay open until 11, which is rarer than it should be in this neighborhood.
