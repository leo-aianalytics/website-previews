Our Story

A quiet corner where Columbus Ave slows down

There's a stretch of Columbus Ave where the Financial District starts giving way to something older and more human — where the foot traffic thins out and you notice the details again. That's where AL Blades landed, at number 13, a small shop that keeps short hours and does the work right. The schedule is tight: four days a week, three hours each evening, which tells you something about the priorities here. This isn't about volume. It's about showing up when it counts.

The craft at AL Blades is the kind that doesn't need much explaining. You sit down, you say what you want — or maybe you've been coming long enough that you don't even need to say it — and the work speaks for itself. The late afternoon light comes in off Columbus, the chair is good, and the conversation is whatever you need it to be that day. Some barbers cut hair. AL cuts it like it matters.

People come back to AL Blades for the same reason they go back to any place that does one thing exceptionally well: it feels right every single time. In a city that's always moving and changing, there's something genuinely grounding about a place with consistent hours, a steady hand, and a barber who remembers what worked for you last time. It's a small shop on a short schedule — and somehow, that's exactly enough.

"I've tried probably six barbers since moving to SF and kept bouncing around. A coworker pointed me toward AL Blades and I've been going back for over a year now. The hours are a little unusual — evenings only, Monday through Thursday — but once you plan around it, it's honestly fine. The cut is consistently great. He listens, doesn't overdo it, and the fade is always cleaner than I expect. Worth the trip down Columbus."

What Happens in the Chair

Clean, focused barbering — done the way it should be, every time

Haircut

A proper haircut that actually matches what you described — not a rushed approximation of it. AL takes the time to get the shape right, whether you want something tight and clean or a little more relaxed.

Fade & Taper

The kind of fade that holds up in photos and holds up in real life. It's blended the old-fashioned way — by hand and by eye — with the detail work that separates a good barber from a great one.

Beard Trim & Shape

Your beard gets the same attention as your hair — defined lines, balanced shape, and none of that uneven patchwork that drives you crazy two days later. Come in looking rough, leave looking intentional.
