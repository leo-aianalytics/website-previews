Our Story

A little piece of old-world baking on Balboa Street

Tucked between 5th and 6th Avenue on Balboa, Cinderella has the kind of storefront you almost walk past — and then the smell stops you. Fresh bread, something buttery and warm, maybe a hint of cinnamon. It's been that way for a long time. The Inner Richmond has changed around it — new cafes, new restaurants, whole new waves of residents — but Cinderella has stayed put, doing what it does, day after day, seven days a week from 7 in the morning.

The baking here draws on Russian and Eastern European traditions that don't cut corners. The pirozhki are stuffed generously. The cakes are layered the way they're supposed to be. There's a particular care in the way everything is made that you don't find in a place that's just going through the motions — you can taste the difference between a recipe that's been handed down and one that's been scaled up for efficiency.

People come in on their way to work and grab a pastry and a coffee. They come on Saturday mornings with their kids. They come when they're homesick for something that tastes like Eastern Europe, or just when they want something that feels real. A lot of businesses promise that kind of experience. Cinderella just delivers it, quietly, every single day.

"I've been coming here since I moved to the Inner Richmond eight years ago and it genuinely never disappoints. The pirozhki with cabbage and egg is my standing order — I've tried everything else on the menu but I always come back to that. The staff recognize me at this point which honestly makes my week every time."

What Comes Out of the Oven

Traditional Russian and European baking made fresh daily — bread, pastries, cakes, and cafe drinks done simply and well

Fresh-Baked Breads & Pastries

Every morning the cases fill up with pirozhki, piroshki, and breads made from recipes that predate trend cycles by a few generations. Grab one warm with a coffee and it's hard to imagine a better start to a San Francisco morning.

Russian & European Specialty Cakes

Whole cakes and slices that lean into the Eastern European tradition — layered, rich, and made with care rather than shortcuts. Whether you're picking one up for a birthday or just treating yourself on a Tuesday, these are cakes worth sitting down for.

Cafe Drinks & Light Bites

The cafe side is simple and honest — good coffee, tea, and enough savory bites to make it a proper lunch stop. It's the kind of place where you can sit for a bit and feel like you're not in a rush, even when you are.
