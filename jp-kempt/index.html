Our Story

How a barbershop became a neighborhood fixture

There's a stretch of Divisadero that hums on a Saturday morning — coffee cups, dogs on leashes, the sound of someone's bike lock clicking shut. J.P. Kempt sits right in the middle of it, at 351 Divisadero, quietly doing what it's always done: giving people a great haircut without making a big deal about it. Walk in and you'll immediately understand why regulars treat this place like a standing appointment with an old friend.

The barbers here are the real thing — people who actually care about the craft, who'll ask what you want and listen when you answer. They're not rushing you out the door to hit a quota. The $$$-tier pricing reflects the level of attention you get: a considered cut, a real conversation, scissors in the hands of someone who knows what they're doing.

People come once because they need a haircut, and they come back because it felt like the right place. There's something rare about a shop that has 290-plus reviews and still feels personal, still feels local. That doesn't happen by accident. It happens because the people behind the chair genuinely give a damn — and in a neighborhood like this, that's worth a lot.

"I've been going to J.P. Kempt for almost two years now and I genuinely can't imagine going anywhere else.
