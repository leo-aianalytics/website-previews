Our Story

Started because someone got tired of bad açai

Deep Purpl showed up on New Montgomery Street between Stevenson and Jessie a few years back, born from a pretty simple frustration: the founders kept ordering açai bowls that tasted more like dessert than breakfast. So they decided to do it right—sourcing real açai, piling on fruit that actually ripens, and building a little counter where regulars started recognizing each other by their usual order.

The magic isn't in some secret recipe or fancy equipment. It's in the consistency. Every bowl gets the same care, whether you're ordering at 8am before work or swinging by at 6pm after the gym. The team knows their fruit like a bartender knows their spirits—when the strawberries are good, when to switch suppliers, how to build a bowl so the granola doesn't get soggy before you take the first bite.

Over 66 reviews on Yelp, and people keep coming back because this isn't a chain—it's a place that remembers you and respects your time. You walk in, order something that's actually nourishing, and leave feeling like you made a good choice. That matters more than people think.

What We Do

Fresh bowls and cold-pressed juice made the way you'd make them at home, if you had the time and fruit budget.

Açai Bowls

A real açai base topped with granola, coconut, fresh berries, and whatever else you want piled on. Every bowl is built fresh in front of you—no pre-made batches sitting under heat lamps. This is the kind of breakfast that actually keeps you full.

Fresh Juices & Smoothies

Cold-pressed to order or blended thick depending on what you're after. Whether you need a morning green juice or a post-workout protein smoothie, nothing here is watered down or overly sweet. Just fruit, greens, and ingredients you'd recognize.

Food Delivery (DoorDash, UberEats)

Can't make it in? Deep Purpl delivers to the neighborhood. Bowls travel well if you're nearby, and they time it so your toppings stay crispy and your açai stays cold. Not the same as eating at the counter, but close enough when you're slammed.
